# NTIPL Email Signature Setup Guide

This guide provides step-by-step instructions on how to create, customize, and install your official NTIPL email signature.

---

## Step 1: Upload Your Profile Photo to Google Drive

To display your photo in the signature, it needs to be hosted online with public access. Google Drive is the recommended option.

1. Open **[Google Drive](https://drive.google.com)**.
2. Upload your professional profile photo (recommended format: `.jpg` or `.png`, square aspect ratio).
3. Once uploaded, locate the file in your Google Drive.

---

## Step 2: Make the Photo Accessible to Everyone (Critical)

By default, files uploaded to Google Drive are restricted. You must make the link public so anyone receiving your emails can see your photo.

1. **Right-click** on the uploaded photo.
2. Select **Share** ➔ **Share**.
3. Under the **General access** section, change the setting from **Restricted** to **"Anyone with the link"**.
4. Ensure the role is set to **Viewer** (default).
5. Click the **Copy link** button.
6. Click **Done**.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> If you skip this step or leave the link restricted, your signature photo will appear as a broken image link to external recipients.

---

## Step 3: Generate Your Signature

Now, use the NTIPL signature generator to build your custom signature.

1. Open a web browser and go to the **[NTIPL Signature Hub](https://yashpimpalkar.github.io/ntiplsignature)**.
2. Fill in your professional details in the input fields:
   * **Full Name** (e.g., `John Doe`)
   * **Designation / Job Title** (e.g., `Senior Systems Engineer`)
   * **Phone Number** (e.g., `+91 98765 43210`)
   * **Email Address** (e.g., `john.doe@neotech.com`)
   * **Profile Image URL**: Paste the Google Drive link you copied in Step 2.
3. Review the real-time preview of your signature on the screen to ensure all details are correct.
4. Click the **Copy Signature** button. A confirmation message will appear, and the styled signature will be copied to your clipboard.

> [!TIP]
> The NTIPL Signature Hub automatically processes your Google Drive link to extract the correct direct image hosting URL. You don't need to manually modify the link.

---

## Step 4: Add Your Signature to Your Email Client

Choose the instructions below depending on which email client you use:

### Option A: Microsoft Outlook

1. Open **Microsoft Outlook** (Desktop app).
2. Go to **File** ➔ **Account Info** ➔ **Mail** ➔ **Compose**.
3. Check that **Compose messages in this format** is set to **HTML**. If it is already set to HTML, you’re good to proceed.
4. Go to **Signature**.
5. Click **+ New signature** and give it a name (e.g., *Official NTIPL*).
6. Click inside the signature text editor box.
7. Paste the signature from your clipboard (**Ctrl + V** on Windows, **Cmd + V** on Mac).
8. Under **Select default signatures**, choose your new signature for:
   * *New messages*
   * *Replies/forwards*
9. Click **Save** at the bottom of the page.


---

### Option B: Zimbra Mail Client

1. Log in to the **Zimbra Web Client**.
2. Click on the **Preferences** tab at the top.
3. In the left sidebar, click on **Signatures**.
4. Click **Add Signature** (or edit your existing one).
5. Give your signature a name.
6. **Crucial Zimbra Step:** In the top right corner of the signature editor box, change the dropdown format from **"Format as Plain Text"** to **"Format as HTML"**.
7. Click inside the text area and paste the signature (**Ctrl + V** or **Cmd + V**).
8. Under the **Using Signatures** section (below the editor), set your default signature:
   * Choose your new signature for *New messages* and *Replies/forwards*.
   * Select whether to place the signature above or below the email thread (above is recommended).
9. Click **Save** in the top-left corner of the Zimbra Preferences screen.

## Troubleshooting & FAQs

| Issue | Cause | Solution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Photo is not showing up (broken image icon)** | The Google Drive link is restricted or the URL is incorrect. | Repeat **Step 2** and make sure "Anyone with the link" is selected. Check if the link was pasted fully. |
| **Signature formatting looks messy after pasting** | The email client editor is set to plain text mode. | Ensure your editor is set to **HTML** mode (especially in Zimbra). |
| **Font colors or alignments look incorrect** | Some email clients strip out styling when pasting. | Try using a different browser (Chrome/Edge/Firefox) to copy and paste the signature. |
